## Summary
This video teaches how to answer high-level product strategy questions like "What should Company X build next?" or "You're the CEO of Company Y - what's your 5-year roadmap?" These questions test big-picture, long-term thinking and are distinct from product sense questions. The three-part framework covers understanding the company (mission, investments, strengths, goals), analyzing ecosystem opportunities (users, trends, competitors), and developing execution plans (narrative, roadmap, risks).

## Key Takeaways
1. Strategy questions test CEO-level thinking about big-picture, long-term direction
2. Companies want PMs who can think ambitiously about next big ideas (mobile, metaverse, cloud, AI/AR/VR)
3. Innovation can come from new technologies OR novel applications of existing technologies
4. Start by deeply understanding company mission, current investments, and core strengths
5. Identify 3-5 opportunity areas, then prioritize based on criteria aligned with company goals
6. Strong execution section includes strategic narrative (investor pitch), 2-5 year roadmap with milestones, immediate next steps, and risk mitigation
7. This framework differs from "Should Company X acquire Company Y?" questions (separate video needed)

## Frameworks & Techniques Demonstrated
### Product Strategy Framework (3-Part Structure)

**Part 1 - The Company**
1. **Mission:** What does the company care about? (e.g., Google: organize world's information; Facebook: build community)
2. **Investments:** What spaces is the company currently investing in? (e.g., Google: Android OS, Pixel devices, Wi-Fi routers, Google Home, Search, Ads platform)
3. **Strengths:** What are 3-5 core strengths to leverage? (e.g., Google's search engine dominance â†’ YouTube's strong search algorithms)
4. **Goal:** What is the company optimizing for?
   - Growing new users
   - Engaging existing users
   - Monetizing existing users
   - Cutting costs
   - Innovating without concrete monetization plan

**Part 2 - Ecosystem & Opportunities**
1. **Users:** Who to focus on depends on goal:
   - New users: Who's not using products?
   - Monetization: Who has willingness to pay?
   - Engagement: Who are existing users?
   - Innovation: What gigantic problems exist?
   - Cost-cutting: Which org areas contribute highest costs?
   - Choose users comprising large groups (think big)

2. **Big Gaps:** What are the largest gaps these users experience? (not just general gaps)

3. **Trends:** What growing trends relate to these spaces? Mix trends for innovation

4. **Competitors:** Who are competitors in this space?
   - What successful strategies did they use to grow/monetize?
   - Use as inspiration to copy or improve upon

5. **Opportunity Areas:** Brainstorm 3-5 opportunity areas, then prioritize based on:
   - Big need for target users
   - Solves for the goal
   - Leverages company strengths
   - Take time to think silently before answering

**Part 3 - Execution**
1. **Strategic Narrative:** Investor pitch-style story including:
   - Who you're building for
   - What big gaps you're solving
   - How it aligns with company mission
   - What strengths you're leveraging

2. **Roadmap:** 2-5 year plan broken down by 6-month or yearly milestones
   - What to accomplish at each stage
   - Shows long-term thinking and process

3. **Getting Started:** What will you do in next 6 months?
   - List immediate action steps

4. **Risks:** What risks do you anticipate?
   - Show ability to think ahead
   - Demonstrate plan to prevent failure

## Common Mistakes Highlighted
- Confusing strategy questions with product sense/execution questions
- Not understanding the difference between broad ambiguous strategy vs narrower product design
- Failing to ground strategy in company mission and strengths
- Choosing users/opportunities not aligned with stated company goal
- Not thinking big enough (missing CEO-level ambition)
- Weak strategic narrative that doesn't resemble investor pitch
- Missing the execution section entirely
- Not anticipating risks and mitigation plans
