# Strategy Evaluation Rubric

## Dimensions

### 1. Market Understanding (Weight: 25%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No awareness of market dynamics, competitors, or trends |
| 2 | Surface-level market knowledge, misses key competitors or trends |
| 3 | Solid understanding of market landscape, identifies key players and dynamics |
| 4 | Deep competitive intelligence, quantifies market opportunity (TAM/SAM/SOM) |
| 5 | Reveals non-obvious market insights, anticipates industry shifts, understands regulatory environment |

### 2. Strategic Thinking (Weight: 25%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Reactive, tactical thinking only; no long-term perspective |
| 2 | Some strategic framing but shallow, misses key strategic levers |
| 3 | Clear strategic logic, considers competitive positioning and differentiation |
| 4 | Multi-horizon thinking (now/next/later), identifies sustainable competitive advantages |
| 5 | Visionary strategic perspective with clear competitive moats and platform/ecosystem thinking |

### 3. Business Model Reasoning (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No consideration of revenue, costs, or business sustainability |
| 2 | Mentions revenue but lacks depth on unit economics or monetization strategy |
| 3 | Clear business model analysis, understands revenue streams and cost structure |
| 4 | Sophisticated business model analysis with unit economics and margin considerations |
| 5 | Full financial reasoning including CAC/LTV, network effects, and scaling dynamics |

### 4. Risk Assessment (Weight: 15%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Ignores risks entirely, presents only the upside |
| 2 | Acknowledges risks exist but does not analyze them |
| 3 | Identifies 2-3 key risks with likelihood and impact assessment |
| 4 | Comprehensive risk matrix with specific mitigation strategies |
| 5 | Proactive risk identification including black swan scenarios, regulatory risks, and competitive responses |

### 5. Actionability (Weight: 15%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Abstract ideas with no path to execution |
| 2 | Some concrete steps but no clear timeline or resource needs |
| 3 | Clear action plan with phases and key milestones |
| 4 | Detailed roadmap with resource requirements, dependencies, and success criteria |
| 5 | Executable plan with contingencies, stakeholder alignment strategy, and measurable checkpoints |

## Follow-up Probe Suggestions

- "What would [competitor] do in response to this strategy?"
- "How would you size this market opportunity?"
- "What is the biggest risk to this strategy and how would you mitigate it?"
- "How would you convince the CEO to invest in this direction?"
- "What would make you abandon this strategy?"
