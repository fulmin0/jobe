# Behavioral Evaluation Rubric

## Dimensions

### 1. Story Selection (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Irrelevant story, does not address the competency being assessed |
| 2 | Loosely related story, low stakes or limited demonstration of the skill |
| 3 | Relevant story that demonstrates the competency, reasonable complexity |
| 4 | Strong story with high stakes and clear demonstration of the exact competency |
| 5 | Perfect story selection â€” high-impact, recent, and uniquely demonstrates the competency |

### 2. STAR Structure (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No structure, rambling narrative with no clear beginning/middle/end |
| 2 | Some structure but unbalanced (too much situation, too little action) |
| 3 | Clear STAR structure with appropriate proportions (~10% S, 10% T, 60% A, 20% R) |
| 4 | Tight STAR with smooth transitions, each section adds value |
| 5 | Natural storytelling that follows STAR without feeling mechanical |

### 3. Personal Contribution (Weight: 25%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Uses "we" throughout, no clarity on personal role or decisions |
| 2 | Mentions personal role but actions are vague ("I helped with...") |
| 3 | Clear personal actions and decisions, uses "I" for specific contributions |
| 4 | Deep detail on personal thought process, decision-making, and specific actions taken |
| 5 | Shows leadership through personal initiative, influence on others, and driving outcomes |

### 4. Impact and Results (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No results mentioned, story trails off without resolution |
| 2 | Vague results ("it went well", "the project succeeded") |
| 3 | Specific, quantified results with clear connection to personal actions |
| 4 | Impressive results with business impact, includes both immediate and longer-term outcomes |
| 5 | Exceptional results with multiple impact dimensions (team, business, customer, process) |

### 5. Self-Awareness and Growth (Weight: 15%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No reflection, presents everything as perfect |
| 2 | Token "what I learned" that feels rehearsed |
| 3 | Genuine reflection on what went well and what could improve |
| 4 | Insightful lessons with evidence of behavioral change in subsequent situations |
| 5 | Deep self-awareness, connects learning to personal growth trajectory and leadership philosophy |

## Follow-up Probe Suggestions

- "What would you do differently if you faced this situation again?"
- "How did the other stakeholders react? How did you handle disagreement?"
- "What was the most difficult decision you had to make in this situation?"
- "Can you give me a specific example of how you influenced [person/team]?"
- "What did you learn from this experience that you've applied since?"
- "Tell me about a time this approach didn't work. What did you do?"
